# FossilFrame  
**Quantum-Inspired Computational Imaging for Fossil Reconstruction**

## Overview  
FossilFrame is a hybrid computational pipeline designed to enhance fossil images by recovering structural details lost due to the physical limitations of traditional optical microscopy. By integrating classical image processing, quantum-inspired simulation, and machine learning, the system reconstructs higher-quality representations from blurred inputs.

## Core Concept  
Optical microscopes are fundamentally limited by the diffraction limit, which prevents the resolution of extremely fine structures. As a result, a portion of critical biological and structural information remains inaccessible.

FossilFrame addresses this limitation by combining:
- Classical optical simulation  
- Quantum-inspired probabilistic modeling  
- AI-driven reconstruction  

to recover and enhance hidden features within fossil images.

---

## System Architecture  

### 1. Classical Imaging Simulation  
Applies diffraction blur and noise to emulate the limitations of optical microscopy using OpenCV.

### 2. Quantum-Inspired Simulation  
Utilizes Qiskit to generate probabilistic photon distributions that encode sub-visible structural information.

### 3. Phase Map Generation  
Transforms quantum measurement outputs into phase-like representations to highlight hidden patterns.

### 4. AI-Based Reconstruction  
Leverages deep learning models (TensorFlow/PyTorch) to reconstruct enhanced images from the generated phase data.
